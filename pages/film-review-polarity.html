I was studing a Certificate of Higher Education about multiplatform developement when I met Emilio, my teacher of Android and Python. And I have to say that he was one of the first figures in the software world for whom I felt a strong admiration. He amazed me with the ease with which he solved problems and broke down complex concepts in simple explanations

At that time, he was working on research in a group at the University of Jaume I. His research was primarily focused on deep/machine learning models. He explained to me what was going on there and aroused my great interest, so I decided to do a mini-project to filter tweets and classify the sentiment of the tweet (positive or negative). The tweets were classified with an implementation of Naive Bayes. (Very amateur at the time, but it worked acceptably)

As I was happy with the result I decided to continue with this topic for my final project. I wanted to do something for classify sentiments in Spanish language. But there was a lack of Spanish databases. Then I started building my own one. I did web scrapping in filmaffinity during a few days and I obtained 150000 reviews with scores. I am not sharing the script for two reason, the code is horrible and it no longer works due to changes in the website
